Prof. Albert Einstein

Genius of modern physics.

Here's a time line of Prof. Albert Einstein's life:

  • March 14, 1879: Born in Ulm, Kingdom of Württemberg, German Empire
  • 1884: Moves to Munich, Bavaria
  • 1885: Begins education at a Catholic elementary school
  • 1891: Enters the Luitpold Gymnasium in Munich
  • 1894: Moves to Switzerland to attend the Swiss Federal Polytechnic University
  • 1896: Begins studying physics at the Swiss Federal Polytechnic University
  • 1900: Graduates with a degree in physics
  • 1902: Completes his Ph.D. thesis on the photoelectric effect
  • 1902-1909: Works as a patent clerk in Bern, Switzerland
  • 1905: Annus Mirabilis (Miracle Year) - publishes four groundbreaking papers
  • 1908: Becomes a lecturer at the University of Bern
  • 1911: Becomes a professor at the University of Prague
  • 1914: Returns to Germany as a professor at the University of Berlin
  • 1915: Develops the General Theory of Relativity
  • 1920s-1950s: Works on the Unified Field Theory
  • 1905: Special Theory of Relativity (Introduced the famous equation E=mc²)
  • 1905: Photoelectric Effect Explanation (Showed that light can behave as particles (photons))
  • 1905: Brownian Motion Explanation (Provided strong evidence for the existence of atoms and molecules)
  • 1915: General Theory of Relativity (Revolutionized our understanding of gravity and spacetime)
  • 1916: Gravitational Waves Prediction (Proposed the existence of ripples in spacetime)
  • 1917: Cosmology and the Expanding Universe (Introduced the concept of an expanding universe)
  • 1920-1950: Unified Field Theory (Attempted to unify fundamental forces (gravity, electromagnetism, strong and weak nuclear forces))
  • 1924: Bose-Einstein Statistics (Described the behavior of bosons (particles with integer spin))
  • 1935: EPR Paradox (Challenged the principles of quantum mechanics)
  • 1921: Nobel Prize in Physics (Awarded for his explanation of the photoelectric effect)
  • 1903: Marries Mileva Marić
  • 1904: Has first son, Hans Albert Einstein
  • 1910: Has second son, Eduard Einstein
  • 1919: Divorces Mileva Marić
  • 1919: Marries Elsa Löwenthal
  • 1933: Emigrates to the United States due to Nazi threat
  • 1933-1955: Works at Princeton University
  • 1940: Becomes a U.S. citizen
  • April 18, 1955: Passes away in Princeton, New Jersey

"Einstein was a giant, and his name will live in human memory for centuries to come."

— J. Robert Oppenheimer
